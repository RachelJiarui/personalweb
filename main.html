About Me

My name is Rachel Chen. I'm a first-year student studying a Bachelor's of Science degree in Computer Science and Mathematics at Northeastern University.

I grew up loving the sciences, and throughout my early education, I participated in local science camps such as KTByte and Idea Math Camp. In junior year of high school, I applied for MIT PRIMES where I was accepted into the Computer Science Research track with Jules Drean, a graduate student at MIT CSAIL, as my advisor. Through the two years under his mentorship, we built together a new kind of technology: Non-Fungible Objects (NFO): Hard-to-Counterfeit Virtual Assets Based On Trusted-Hardware. In my junior year summer, I applied and was accepted into the Hampshire College Summer Studies in Mathematics, a six-week intensive summer studies where I fell in love with mathematics and met some incredible people along the way.

Now in college, I'm working on learning Full Stack Web Development, taking on projects such as this personal website and an interactive boston map web application at Oasis this fall 2022. Currently, I'm learning more about how to develop the back-end; From learning about database queries to gathering data, I hope to expand my knowledge through these projects. I look forward to using my skills in hackathons and developing my own ideas into reality. You will be able to track my projects and progress on this exact website!
